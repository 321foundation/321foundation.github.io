Building a strong foundation for children


Around 130 million school-going children in India study in primary grades. But, 7 out of 10 children have not attained the foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN) skills required for success in higher studies. As a result, they cannot advance as productive citizens of the country.

We aim to ensure the attainment of foundational learning outcomes for over half a million children by 2025.

Our Work

Bharat EdTech Initiative

2022 - present

We are community partners of a nation-wide initiative that aims to enhance learning outcomes for first-generation digital learners by leveraging at-home learning time through proven EdTech solutions.

FLN program: PARaM

2021 - present

We are directly impacting the learning outcomes of 18000 students in 450 classrooms across 6 cities in India. We do this by building the capacity of 500 teachers through a hybrid delivery model.

Online resource banks: CAPE & E-Shaala

2020 - 2021

We helped 15000 teachers in 1000 schools across the under-resourced communities of India with ready-to-use lessons to improve at-home learning for 300000 students during COVID-19-related school closures.

Collaboration with Govt. of NCT of Delhi

2020 - 2021

We trained teachers in the core academic unit to enhance content creation process and facilitated content distribution for 12 lakh students. We also conducted workshops over 6 days for trainers from DIET, SCERT to improve the facilitation skills of the participants.

FLN Programs: Ignite 3.0 & i-Ignite

2018 - 2021

Tripled the % of students achieving grade level outcomes in numeracy and literacy in 72 schools across three cities. We could also help the same schools sustain these learning gains through the pandemic.

Capacity Building Programs: Ignite 1.0 & 2.0

2016 - 2019

Improved teaching-learning practices of 2500 teachers across 80 schools in Mumbai, Hyderabad & Bengaluru. An independent evaluation showed that there was 18% growth in teachers performance between 2017-2019.

3.2.1 School

2012 - 2016

Holistically improved learning outcomes for 200 students in Mumbai. The learnings and best practices derived at school laid the foundations for all our future programs. The school was then transitioned to the Akanksha Foundation.
